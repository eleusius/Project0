From a Psychological perspective, this idea builds on the theories of the mind put forth by Carl G. Jung, which propose that the psyche has three layers - a conscious layer, a personal unconscious layer, and a collective unconscious layer.

The conscious layer corresponds to the material world we can observe, and to our “brain states.” While the personal unconscious layer corresponds to the non-material, or “dark matter” layer of the Universe, in a similar way to how matter seems to be just the “tip of an iceberg” in the gravitational domain of a larger dark matter “halo.” And the collective unconscious layer corresponds to the “dark energy” solution of the Universe, which is in a sense “static” or “neutral.” Jung’s “archetypes” therefore and perhaps Plato’s “Ideas” correspond to this layer of reality which describes some sort of “universal” for each “particular” state of mind or matter.
